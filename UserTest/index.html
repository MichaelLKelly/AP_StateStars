Every Purple Flaw

It is common in the U.S. today to readily speak of 'red,' 'purple,' and 'blue' states, as if these are monoliths with the same characteristics. Similar presumptions are made when residents speak of regions such as 'the Coasts' or 'the South,' 'the Rust Belt' or 'the Bible Belt.' In fact, all states are mixtures of blue and red, and shared characteristics exist between the deepest red and the brightest blue.

With this in mind, this project presents a series of state portraits, each created using rankings (50th to 1st) from the 2021 U.S. News and World Reports Best States platform, and supplemented by data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Bureau of Economic Analysis. Each portrait consists of a 'Purple Value' and five star points, with larger points being indicative of a higher ranking — and therefore generally pereceived as 'more desirable' for residents.


You are encouraged to read through, examine the relationships presented here and create some comparisons of your own to find links where they were perhaps not expected. You will no doubt also find that no star in the union is perfect.

Sources and Inspiration:

To better understand the methodology behind the Best States platform and the categories utilized, please visit the U.S. News website. Supplementary data was taken from the U.S. Census Bureau and the U.S. Bureau of Econimic Analysis. Except where noted, all data is primarily from 2019 numbers.

The initial Purple Value is from the Cook Political Report's Partisan Voting Index

The color scheme and other inspiration come from America the Beautiful, by Katharine Lee Bates. You can read more about her life here and check out Ray Charles' doing it justice here.
